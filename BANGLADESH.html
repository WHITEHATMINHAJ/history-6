Historical Timeline

A journey through the rich historical tapestry of Bengal

Ancient Bengal

Bengal was part of large ancient kingdoms such as Vanga, Pundra, and Gauda. The region had prosperous trade links with the Roman Empire, Southeast Asia, and other regions.

Medieval Period

During the Mughal period, Bengal was one of the wealthiest parts of the empire, generating over 50% of the empire's GDP and 12% of the world's GDP.

British Colonial Era

Bengal came under British control after the Battle of Plassey in 1757. The region played a significant role in India's independence movement.

Partition & Pakistan Era

In 1947, Bengal was partitioned, with western Bengal becoming part of India and eastern Bengal becoming East Pakistan.

Liberation War

The Bangladesh Liberation War in 1971 led to independence from Pakistan after nine months of conflict.

Modern Bangladesh

Since independence, Bangladesh has transitioned from a war-torn nation to a developing economy with impressive growth metrics.

Welcome to Bangladesh

Discover the Beauty

Bangladesh, located in South Asia, is a country of vibrant culture, rich history, and breathtaking natural beauty. Known as the "Land of Rivers," it features the world's largest river delta formed by the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Meghna rivers.

With a population of over 170 million people, Bangladesh is the eighth most populous country in the world and has one of the fastest-growing economies globally.

  • Home to the world's largest mangrove forest (Sundarbans)
  • Longest natural sea beach (Cox's Bazar)
  • Rich cultural heritage with six distinct seasons
  • Rapidly developing economy with strong agricultural and manufacturing sectors
Bangladesh Economy - Detailed Report

Economic Overview

Bangladesh has maintained an impressive economic growth trajectory over the past decade, with GDP growth consistently exceeding 6% annually. The country has transformed from one of the poorest nations at independence to a lower-middle-income country.

Key Economic Indicators
  • GDP (Nominal): $465 billion (2023 estimate)
  • GDP (PPP): $1.1 trillion (2023 estimate)
  • GDP Growth: 6.4% (2022-23)
  • GDP Per Capita: $2,800 (Nominal), $6,600 (PPP)
  • Inflation: 4.5% (2023)
  • Unemployment: 4.4% (2023)

Major Economic Sectors

Ready-Made Garments (RMG)

Bangladesh is the world's second-largest apparel exporter after China, accounting for 84% of the country's total exports. The sector employs over 4 million workers, mostly women.

Agriculture

Despite rapid industrialization, agriculture remains a significant sector, employing about 40% of the workforce. Bangladesh is self-sufficient in rice production and a major producer of jute, tea, and tropical fruits.

Services Sector

The services sector contributes over 53% to GDP, with IT, telecommunications, and financial services showing rapid growth.

Future Outlook

Bangladesh aims to become an upper-middle-income country by 2031 and a developed country by 2041. Key focus areas include infrastructure development, human resource development, and economic diversification.
